# Evidence Integration & Synthesis
# Bringing together all four types of evidence to inform your decision

## Bayesian Evidence Synthesis Framework

### Core Hypothesis (A): 
**"Implementing shared governance and participatory communication will improve staff engagement, reduce turnover, and enhance organizational performance 
in our 210-bed critical access hospital"**

### Bayesian Formula Applied: P(A|B) = [P(B|A) × P(A)] / P(B)

**Where:**
- **P(A|B)** = Posterior probability (updated belief after evidence)
- **P(B|A)** = Likelihood (probability of observing evidence given hypothesis is true)
- **P(A)** = Prior probability (initial belief before evidence)
- **P(B)** = Evidence probability (probability of observing this evidence regardless)

## Evidence-Based Problem Refinement

### Original Problem Statement
Top-down communication structures limit staff critical thinking and problem-solving, resulting in decreased engagement and increased turnover.

### Evidence-Refined Problem Statement
**Communication barriers in our 210-bed critical access hospital create a cascade of organizational performance challenges:** hierarchical decision-making 
structures → staff disengagement (2.9/5.0 engagement score) → increased turnover (22.1% vs. 14.6% national benchmark) → elevated costs ($1.247M annually) → 
compromised patient satisfaction (68.2% HCAHPS communication) → safety risks (35% of incidents involve communication failures). **The evidence strongly 
supports that shared governance implementation can reverse this cascade:** participatory communication → improved engagement → reduced turnover 
(15-30% reduction) → cost savings $450K+ annually) → enhanced patient satisfaction → improved safety outcomes.

**Key Refinements:**
- **Scope Changes:** Problem extends beyond communication to comprehensive organizational performance system
- **Root Cause Updates:** Evidence identifies hierarchical structures as primary cause with multiple downstream effects
- **Impact Reassessment:** Financial impact ($1.247M) and patient safety implications (35% incident involvement) greater than initially estimated
- **Stakeholder Effects:** Management resistance (43%) and implementation timeline (12-18 months) more significant factors than initially recognized
